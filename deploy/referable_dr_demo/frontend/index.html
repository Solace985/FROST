How common is diabetic retinopathy?

Pooled global prevalence among people with diabetes — meta-analysis of 35 studies, Yau et al. (Diabetes Care, 2012; n ≈ 22,900).

  • Any DR: any diabetic retinopathy
  • VTDR: vision-threatening DR
  • PDR: proliferative DR
  • DME: diabetic macular edema

Referable DR (moderate non-proliferative DR or worse) targets exactly the vision-threatening end of this spectrum — the cases where timely referral matters most.

      FROST — referable-DR research demonstrator

      About & study basis

      FROST (Frozen Representation for Ocular Screening and Triage) reproduces, for local inspection, the referable diabetic retinopathy pipeline from the underlying study. The study itself is defined in the manuscript; FROST is a demonstrator built on top of it.

      • Dataset: BRSET, a Brazilian colour-fundus dataset; results are from BRSET’s internal distribution. Details accessible at: A Brazilian Multilabel Ophthalmological Dataset (BRSET) v1.0.1.
      • Cohort & split: a patient-level train / validation / reliability / test split (no patient appears in two splits).
      • Referable DR: defined as DR grade ≥ 2 (moderate non-proliferative DR or worse).
      • Model: a frozen RETFound-Green backbone at its native 392×392 protocol (average pooling, 384-dimensional embedding) feeding a lightweight multi-task head. The backbone receives no gradient updates.
      • Why this model: it was selected for the demonstrator for its strong internal referable-DR performance with a compact frozen backbone not a claim of universal superiority over larger models.
      • Uncertainty: the study reported patient-clustered bootstrap confidence intervals as study-level evidence; these are not per-image confidence.
      • Operating point: the decision threshold was selected on the validation split only (largest threshold preserving validation sensitivity ≥ 0.95) and then frozen.
